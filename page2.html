What are the Game Awards

The Game Awards is a yearly celebration of the imaginative creativity and innovation that drives the world's most popular gaming industry. Since its inception, the event has existed as a means to honor the excellent work of developers, designers, composers, and writers who continuously challenge the boundaries of interactive entertainment.

History

Launched in 2014, The Game Awards is now one of the most-watched annual entertainment programs, with millions of viewers from across the world. The event includes award ceremonies, world-first game reveals, and live performances that celebrate the ever-evolving world of video games.

My Mission

The Game Awards is dedicated to putting a spotlight on the art of video games—on fresh gameplay, great stories, and technical achievement. Beyond the actual awards, it is dedicated to uniting players, developers, and industry personalities from all around the world in a shared passion for gaming.

Why It Matters

Video games aren't just a hobby—they're an engine of creativity, cultural impact, and connection. The Game Awards is an international stage where the best in games is honored, inspiring the next generation of developers.
